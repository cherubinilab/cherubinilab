Prof. Enrico Cherubini

Biography, Research Interests and Scientific Contributions

Biography

Enrico Cherubini was born in Rome, Italy on Jan 20, 1945. In 1968, after obtaining his degree (M.D) in Medicine and Surgery he spent few months in Dublin working with autistic children at Trinity College under the with Prof. Ingo Fisher. He spent a year in Marseille, France with Prof. H. Gastaut, a leading figure in the field of Epilepsy, he obtained the "Attestation d'Etude d'Electroencephalographie Clinique". After spending several years at the Institute of Child Neurology at the University of Rome where he contributed to build up the Clinical Neurophysiology Unit, in 1981 he decided to dedicate himself exclusively to basic research.

After a period of about 15 years abroad, at UCLA in Los Angeles and at MIT in Boston as post-doctoral researcher and in Paris, as Directeur de Recherche at INSERM as a group leader, he returned to Italy in 1991 as Full Professor of Physiology at SISSA in Trieste. Here he directed the Department of Biophysics and then of Neurobiology for many years. Retired from SISSA, in 2014, he was appointed as the scientific director of European Brain Research Institute in Rome by Rita Levi-Montalcini.

He served on various grants committee at national [CNR, MIUR (Italy)] and international [INSERM, CNRS, ANR, Ministere de la Recherche (France), European Commission, MRC, Oxford (UK), Riken Institute (Japan) and the Human Frontiers Science Program ] levels. He was a member of the Advisory Board of the Institut Pasteur of Montevideo (Uruguay). From 2011 to 2013 he was President of the Italian Society of Neuroscience.

Research Interests

Over the years, he has provided a series of fundamental studies aimed at understanding the functional role of GABAergic signaling early in postnatal development in both physiological and pathological conditions. He discovered the excitatory action of GABA during postnatal development and characterized network driven GABAA-mediated oscillatory activity in the immature hippocampus. He provided important contributions for understanding how ion channels control neuronal excitability and neurotransmission
